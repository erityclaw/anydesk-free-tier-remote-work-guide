Source notes and editorial framing

This site is written as an editorial synthesis. It leans primarily on AnyDesk’s own public pages and license documentation, then translates those details into remote worker risk language.

Editorial note

Claims here are intentionally cautious. Where exact free-tier behavior may vary over time, this site focuses on the higher-confidence takeaway: a personal-use tier is a poor foundation for business-critical remote work.

Official: AnyDesk personal use page

Public positioning for the free personal experience. Used here to support the point that the free tier is framed around personal, not professional, scenarios.

https://anydesk.com/en/solutions/personal-user

Official: AnyDesk license documentation

Used for license tiers, examples of professional use, and the fact that paid licenses advertise uninterrupted remote access and defined connection/device limits.

https://support.anydesk.com/docs/anydesk-licenses

Official: AnyDesk pricing page

Used as supporting context that business use cases are tied to licensed plans rather than the free personal tier.

https://anydesk.com/en/pricing

How to read this site

This is not claiming that every free AnyDesk session will fail. It is arguing that remote workers should not build their day job around a tier whose positioning and feature model are aimed elsewhere.

Strongest conclusion: the issue is less “AnyDesk is bad” and more “AnyDesk Free is the wrong contract for work-dependent remote access.”
